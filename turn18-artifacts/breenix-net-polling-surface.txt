Turn 18 Breenix P6/P7/P8 polling surface inventory
==================================================

Scope read:
- kernel/src/net/mod.rs
- kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs
- kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_mmio.rs
- kernel/src/main.rs
- kernel/src/task/softirqd.rs
- kernel/src/arch_impl/aarch64/exception.rs
- timer grep for NetRx/raise_softirq in timer files

Important nuance:
- The comments in net/mod.rs and net_pci.rs describe a 10ms timer-raised
  NetRx contract, but `rg -n "NetRx|raise_softirq"` over timer files found no
  live timer raise in the current source snapshot. Treat this as a stale or
  incomplete contract risk: the PCI MSI path still suppresses interrupts and
  relies on a later NetRx softirq re-enable path, while the PCI IRQ handler
  itself never raises NetRx.

Timer-driven device polling / timer-gated PCI completion
--------------------------------------------------------

1. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:235-247
   Category: Timer-driven device polling (documented contract; live timer
   raise not found by grep).
   Surface: `net_rx_softirq_handler` calls `process_rx()` and then, on aarch64
   PCI net, calls `net_pci::re_enable_irq()`. The comment says NetRx is raised
   by a timer every 10ms and that the MSI handler does not raise softirq.

2. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs:828-868
   Category: Timer-driven device polling / timer-gated MSI suppression.
   Surface: PCI MSI-X `handle_interrupt()` increments the MSI counter,
   suppresses virtqueue callbacks with `VRING_AVAIL_F_NO_INTERRUPT`, disables
   and clears the GIC SPI, reads the ISR, and leaves both levels suppressed.
   It does not process packets and does not raise `SoftirqType::NetRx`.

3. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs:871-928
   Category: Timer-driven device polling / timer-gated re-enable.
   Surface: `re_enable_irq()` is documented as called by the NetRx softirq
   after draining the used ring. It clears the device interrupt condition,
   clears `NO_INTERRUPT`, checks `used.idx` against `rx_last_used_idx`, and
   either re-suppresses callbacks or re-enables the GIC SPI.

4. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:467-518
   Category: Timer-driven device polling when reached from NetRx, also shared
   by synchronous init/on-demand paths.
   Surface: aarch64 `process_rx()` loops over `net_pci::receive()` or
   `net_mmio::receive()` until no packet remains, then drains deferred TCP TX.
   It is the common ring-drain primitive for timer/softirq, init polling, and
   on-demand ARP polling.

5. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:448-458 plus kernel/src/main.rs:805-816,
   kernel/src/main.rs:871-882, kernel/src/main.rs:940-951
   Category: Timer-driven device polling (idle/hlt-loop variant).
   Surface: x86 `process_rx()` drains e1000 while `can_receive()` remains true.
   Three test-only idle loops call `net::process_rx()` after `enable_and_hlt()`
   as a workaround for softirq timing.

MSI-driven completion (already closer to Linux; keep or preserve shape)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

6. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_mmio.rs:716-745
   Category: MSI-driven completion.
   Surface: MMIO VirtIO net IRQ handler acknowledges the MMIO interrupt status
   and raises `SoftirqType::NetRx`. This matches the broad Linux shape:
   interrupt context defers packet processing to softirq/NAPI context.

7. kernel/src/arch_impl/aarch64/exception.rs:1264-1268
   Category: MSI-driven completion.
   Surface: aarch64 GIC dispatch routes the MMIO net IRQ to
   `net_mmio::handle_interrupt()`.

8. kernel/src/arch_impl/aarch64/exception.rs:1301-1305
   Category: Timer-gated PCI completion gap.
   Surface: aarch64 GIC dispatch routes the PCI net MSI to
   `net_pci::handle_interrupt()`. Unlike the MMIO path, the PCI handler does
   not raise NetRx.

Synchronous init polling
------------------------

9. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:348-389
   Category: Synchronous init polling.
   Surface: network init sends a gateway ARP request and then loops 100 times
   calling `process_rx()`, spinning between iterations, and optionally dumping
   PCI RX state.

10. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:397-417
    Category: Synchronous init polling.
    Surface: after sending an ICMP echo request to the gateway, init loops 20
    times calling `process_rx()` and spinning between iterations.

11. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:421-433
    Category: Synchronous init polling.
    Surface: aarch64 enables PCI MSI-X or MMIO IRQs only after the synchronous
    ARP/ICMP polling loops finish.

12. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs:379-389 and
    kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs:959-984
    Category: Synchronous init polling.
    Surface: PCI MSI-X is configured at PCI level during driver init, but the
    GIC SPI is not enabled until `enable_msi_spi()` runs after init polling.

13. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_mmio.rs:253-284 and
    kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_mmio.rs:690-704
    Category: Synchronous init polling.
    Surface: MMIO net records its IRQ during device init but delays enabling
    it until `enable_net_irq()` after network stack initialization.

Synchronous on-demand resolution
--------------------------------

14. kernel/src/net/mod.rs:596-628
    Category: Synchronous on-demand resolution.
    Surface: on ARP cache miss, Breenix sends an ARP request, loops 50 times
    calling `process_rx()` with spin delays, and either sends after the reply
    arrives or returns an ARP lookup failure.

TX completion busy-wait
-----------------------

15. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_pci.rs:700-747
    Category: TX completion busy-wait.
    Surface: PCI VirtIO net posts a TX descriptor, notifies queue 1, then loops
    on `PCI_TX_QUEUE.used.idx` until it differs from `tx_last_used_idx`, timing
    out after a spin-loop countdown.

16. kernel/src/drivers/virtio/net_mmio.rs:548-588
    Category: TX completion busy-wait.
    Surface: MMIO VirtIO net posts a TX descriptor, notifies queue 1, then
    loops on `TX_QUEUE.used.idx` until it differs from `tx_last_used_idx`,
    timing out after a spin-loop countdown.

Softirq substrate
-----------------

17. kernel/src/task/softirqd.rs:48-82
    Category: MSI-driven completion substrate.
    Surface: `SoftirqType::NetRx` is softirq number 3.

18. kernel/src/task/softirqd.rs:141-145 and kernel/src/task/softirqd.rs:162-224
    Category: MSI-driven completion substrate.
    Surface: `raise_softirq()` marks softirq pending; `do_softirq()` processes
    pending handlers with restart bounds and ksoftirqd fallback. The substrate
    is usable for Linux-shaped IRQ/NAPI-style completion; the PCI driver path
    is the current mismatch.
